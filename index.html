The Brothers Karamazov

Fyodor Dostoevsky

Same story. Different souls.

Two readers. One book. See what each took from it.

Marcus
Favourite Quote
"Above all, don't lie to yourself. The man who lies to himself and listens to his own lie comes to a point that he cannot distinguish the truth."
Favourite Moment

Dmitri breaking down in the courtroom. All that rage and pride finally crumbling. I had to put the book down and sit with it for a while.

Favourite Character

Dmitri Karamazov

The most human character I have ever read. Flawed, loud, irrational — and somehow the most honest of all three brothers.

Thoughts

This book made me sit with guilt in a way I never had before. Dmitri's rage, Ivan's doubt, Alyosha's quiet faith — I saw parts of myself in all three brothers and that was deeply uncomfortable. Dostoevsky doesn't let you pick a side. He makes you live inside all of them.

Lena
Favourite Quote
"It's not miracles that produce faith, but faith that produces miracles."
Favourite Moment

Alyosha holding the dying boy at the end. No words. Just presence. The quietest and most devastating scene in the whole book.

Favourite Character

Ivan Karamazov

Ivan broke me. His rebellion against God isn't atheism — it's grief. The Grand Inquisitor is the most honest thing I've ever read about doubt.

Thoughts

I came for the philosophy and stayed for the grief. The Grand Inquisitor chapter alone changed how I think about freedom and faith. I've never felt so intellectually shattered by a single chapter. And then Alyosha just quietly keeps loving everyone anyway. That contrast destroyed me.
