Vagabond

Takehiko Inoue

Manga
Completed
9/10
A precipitous slope, a narrow river width, a fast and violent flow. Due to the terrain and the external influence, the state of the water is perfectly decided. And yet, water obeys only itself. Water is only water. Thoroughly water. Absolutely free.
Preoccupied with a single leaf, you won’t see the tree. Preoccupied with a single tree, you’ll miss the entire forest. Don’t be preoccupied with a single spot. See everything in its entirety... effortlessly. That is what it means to truly 'see'.
Life has no value! Truly right. It has no value. If you think only of yourself, it has no value. 'Why was I born?' The reason you are here... is because someone supported your life. 'Why was I born? For what purpose?' The reason you are here... is to support lives. If you don't go against how life should be... You're already completely... Free
My sword is one with heaven and earth. That’s why in a fight, I need no sword.
Invincible. It’s merely a word. To be 'invincible.' The more you think about it. The more you squint your eyes in desperation to see, the more obscured the answer becomes. If something’s too obscure to see… Then try closing your eyes. Well…? Do you see how infinite you are?
The strong are always kind.
Words are like the sea. You must descend deep into the waters to see what lies in the bottom. For those content to gaze, there is only beauty and tedium. The truth lies here. How it is accepted is only that: the truth for each man.
What is the meaning of 'strength'? It's to have a mind that does not sway, while continuing to move forward and charge.
What if you choose the wrong path? You must understand that there is more than one path to the top of the mountain.

Chapter 3 — the pacing slows down intentionally. Inoue is making you sit with Musashi instead of rushing you forward.

Kojiro feels peaceful in a way Musashi does not. His silence makes strength feel less like domination and more like presence.

Volume 10 — the fight choreography barely needs words. The page rhythm carries the emotion by itself.

The story keeps asking whether becoming stronger is worth it if you lose the ability to be gentle.

Vagabond made me realise I had been confusing movement with progress.

The contrast between Musashi and Kojiro makes the idea of strength feel much less simple.
